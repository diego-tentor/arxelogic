**Author:** Diego Tentor

**Status:** Exploratory theoretical proposal

**Abstract:** This paper proposes a radical reconceptualization of photons and the speed of light based on ArXe Theory's generative ontology framework. Rather than treating photons as multiple independent particles traveling through space at velocity c, we propose that there exists fundamentally a single excitation act—a unique exentative process—and that what we perceive as "multiple photons moving through space" is actually our relational manifestation within that singular act. ## 1. Introduction: The Mystery of c's Constancy

### 1.1 The Conventional Puzzle

The constancy of the speed of light in vacuum (c ≈ 299,792,458 m/s) for all observers, regardless of their motion, is one of the most counterintuitive features of physical reality. Einstein's special relativity postulates this constancy but does not explain *why* it must be so at a deeper ontological level.

**Standard questions that remain:**
- Why is c the same for all observers?
- Why is there a maximum speed at all?
- Why does light have this special status?

### 1.2 Existing Approaches

Various frameworks have attempted to explain c's constancy:
- **Operational:** c is constant by definition (SI units)
- **Relativistic:** Spacetime structure enforces it (Minkowski geometry)
- **Quantum Field Theory:** Massless particles travel at c
- **Emergent spacetime theories:** c emerges from deeper structure

None fully address *why* this particular structure exists rather than exploring its ontological necessity.

### 1.3 Our Proposal

We propose that **c is constant because there is fundamentally only one excitation act**, and all observers measure their relationship to this same unique act. The photon is not "a particle that moves"; it is the manifestation of the universe's fundamental exentation—its self-alienation process.
